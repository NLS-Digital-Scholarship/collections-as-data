Mortality Statistics.ii 6. Financial.Annual Administration Report of the Civil Vete- rinary Department, Sind, for the year 1937-38. GENERAL ADMINISTRATION. Mr. J. H. G. Jerrom, M.R.C.V.S., I.V.S., held charge throughout the year. He was on tour for 114 days during which period he attended outbreaks of contagious disease when- ever possible and inspected most of the Veterinary Dispensaries. He attended the meeting of the Advisory Board of the Im- perial Council of Agricultural Research held at Delhi. He served as a member of the Board of Examiners of the Bombay Veterinary College. He also acted as Judge at the Jacobabad Horse and Pony Show. In addition, he was on tour in Ajmer- Merwara for 11 days. 2. Mr. C. S. G. Haji continued to hold charge of the Goat Virus Producing Station, Karachi, throughout the year. 3. Mr. H. S. Bawa, M.R.C.V.S., started the work of investigation of diseases in Sind in July 1937. He was on tour for 96 days, travelled 4,588 miles by rail and 1,380 miles by road and visited 53 villages. I.2 tricts and 2 died. The remaining 43 animals were successfully treated. 8.
